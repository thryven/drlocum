<Callout>
Mucolytic agent.
Reduces mucus thickness and helps clear phlegm from the airways.
Used in chronic or acute productive cough.
</Callout>

## Indication

- Productive cough due to respiratory tract infection
- Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
- Chronic bronchitis
- Otitis media with effusion (as adjunct)

## Administration

- Take after meals.
- Drink plenty of fluids to aid mucus clearance.
- Shake syrup well before use.
- Do not use together with cough suppressants.

## Contraindications

- Hypersensitivity to carbocisteine
- Active peptic ulcer
- Children below 2 years (risk of airway obstruction)

## Adverse Reactions

- **Common**
  - Nausea, diarrhoea, stomach pain

- **Uncommon**
  - Rash, itching
  - Vomiting

- **Rare**
  - Gastrointestinal bleeding (especially with ulcer history)

## Pregnancy and Lactation

<Callout>
**Pregnancy:**
Use only if benefits outweigh risks.
Avoid during the first trimester.

**Lactation:**
Limited data.
Use with caution; monitor infant for gastrointestinal upset.
</Callout>
