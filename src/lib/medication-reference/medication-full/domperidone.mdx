<Callout>
  Dopamine receptor antagonist. Increases stomach and intestinal movement. Used
  for nausea, vomiting, and slow gastric emptying.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Nausea and vomiting
- Gastroparesis (delayed stomach emptying)
- Reflux symptoms related to poor gastric motility

### Children

- 0.25 mg/kg 3 times daily before meals
- Maximum: 0.75 mg/kg/day or 30 mg/day (whichever is lower)
- Use only under medical supervision

---

## Administration

- Take 15–30 minutes before meals.
- Swallow tablets whole with water.
- Avoid exceeding 1 week of continuous use without medical advice.

---

## Contraindications

- Known hypersensitivity to domperidone
- Prolonged QT interval or cardiac arrhythmia
- Moderate to severe liver impairment
- Concurrent use of QT-prolonging drugs (e.g. macrolides, azoles)

---

## Adverse Reactions

Common

- Dry mouth
- Headache
- Abdominal cramps

Less common

- QT prolongation, palpitations
- Breast tenderness or galactorrhoea (due to ↑ prolactin)
- Rash or itching

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

<Callout>
**Pregnancy:**
Use only if benefits outweigh risks. Limited data in humans.

**Lactation:**
Excreted in small amounts in breast milk.
Avoid prolonged use; monitor infant for drowsiness or GI symptoms.

</Callout>

---

## Warnings

- Risk of **cardiac arrhythmia and sudden cardiac death** in high doses or prolonged use.
- Use the **lowest effective dose for the shortest possible duration**.
- Caution in elderly or those with heart disease.
