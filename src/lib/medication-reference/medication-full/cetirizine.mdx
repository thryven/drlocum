## Overview

Cetirizine is a non-sedating antihistamine that works by blocking the action of histamine, a substance in the body that causes allergic symptoms. It is effective for treating allergic rhinitis (hay fever), urticaria (hives), and other allergic conditions.

## Indications

- **Allergic Rhinitis**: For the relief of symptoms associated with seasonal and perennial allergic rhinitis, such as sneezing, runny nose, and itchy, watery eyes.
- **Chronic Urticaria**: For the treatment of uncomplicated skin manifestations of chronic idiopathic urticaria.
- **Allergic Conjunctivitis**: To relieve itching and redness of the eyes.

## Dosing

### Pediatric

- **6 to 11 months**: 2.5 mg once daily.
- **12 to 23 months**: 2.5 mg once daily. May be increased to 2.5 mg twice daily.
- **2 to 5 years**: 2.5 mg once daily initially. May be increased to 5 mg once daily or 2.5 mg twice daily.
- **6 years and older**: 5 to 10 mg once daily.

### Adult

- **Oral**: 5 to 10 mg once daily.

## Contraindications

- Known hypersensitivity to cetirizine, hydroxyzine, or any of its components.

## Warnings and Precautions

- **Drowsiness**: Although classified as non-sedating, drowsiness can occur in some patients. Use caution when driving or operating machinery.
- **Renal Impairment**: Dose adjustment is recommended for patients with moderate to severe renal impairment.
- **Hepatic Impairment**: Dose adjustment may be necessary.
- **Geriatric Use**: Elderly patients may be more sensitive to the anticholinergic effects. Use with caution.

## Adverse Effects

Common side effects include:

- Drowsiness
- Fatigue
- Dry mouth
- Pharyngitis
- Dizziness
