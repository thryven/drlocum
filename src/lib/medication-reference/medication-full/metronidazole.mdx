<Callout>
  Active against anaerobic bacteria and certain protozoa. Commonly used for
  intra-abdominal infections, bacterial vaginosis, giardiasis, and as part of
  combination therapy for C. difficile.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Anaerobic bacterial infections, giardiasis, trichomoniasis, bacterial vaginosis, and as indicated in combination regimens.

---

## Dose

- Adults: commonly 400–500 mg every 8–12 hours (oral) depending on indication; single-dose or shorter regimens are used for some conditions.
- Paediatrics: weight-based dosing per product guidance.

---

## Administration

- Avoid alcohol during treatment and for 48 hours after the final dose due to disulfiram-like reaction risk.

---

## Contraindications

<Alert type="error">- Known hypersensitivity to nitroimidazoles.</Alert>

---

## Adverse Reactions

- Nausea, metallic taste, gastrointestinal upset, peripheral neuropathy with prolonged use, and rare hypersensitivity.

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

- Avoid in first trimester unless benefit outweighs risk for some indications; consult local guidance.
