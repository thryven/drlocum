<Callout>
  First-generation antihistamine with prominent sedative properties. Also used
  as an antiemetic for short periods.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Allergic symptoms, short-term management of nausea and vomiting, and as a sedating antihistamine when required.

---

## Dose

- Adults: commonly 10–25 mg every 4–6 hours as needed (maximum daily dose varies by product and jurisdiction).
- Paediatrics: follow product-specific, weight-based dosing and safety guidance; many formulations are not recommended for very young children.

---

## Administration

- Take with caution due to sedation; avoid driving or operating machinery while affected.

---

## Contraindications

<Alert type="error">
  - Hypersensitivity to promethazine. - Comatose states or central nervous
  system depression.
</Alert>

---

## Adverse Reactions

- Sedation, anticholinergic effects (dry mouth, urinary retention), hypotension, extrapyramidal reactions (rare).

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

- Use with caution in pregnancy and lactation; consult local guidance and product SmPC.
