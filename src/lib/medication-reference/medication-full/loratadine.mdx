<Callout>
Second-generation antihistamine.
Relieves allergy symptoms by blocking histamine H₁ receptors.
Non-sedating at usual doses.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Allergic rhinitis (seasonal or perennial)
- Chronic idiopathic urticaria (hives)
- Allergic conjunctivitis
- Skin allergies causing itching

## Administration

- May be taken with or without food.
- Use measuring spoon or oral syringe for liquid.
- Adjust dose in severe liver impairment (every other day).

---

## Contraindications

- Allergy to loratadine or desloratadine
- Severe hepatic impairment (without dose adjustment)
- Caution in renal impairment

---

## Adverse Reactions

- **Common**
  - Headache
  - Drowsiness (rare at usual dose)
  - Dry mouth, fatigue

- **Uncommon**
  - Nausea, gastritis
  - Skin rash

- **Rare**
  - Tachycardia or palpitations
  - Liver enzyme elevation

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

<Callout>
**Pregnancy:**
No evidence of harm in human studies.
Considered safe when clinically needed.

**Lactation:**
Small amount passes into breast milk.
Usually safe; monitor for infant irritability or sleepiness.
</Callout>
