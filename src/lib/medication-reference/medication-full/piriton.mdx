<Callout>
First-generation antihistamine.
Relieves allergy and cold symptoms by blocking histamine H₁ receptors.
May cause drowsiness and dry mouth.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Allergic rhinitis (hay fever, dust allergy)
- Urticaria (hives)
- Allergic skin disorders (itch, rash)
- Common cold (symptomatic relief)

## Administration

- May be taken with or without food.
- Avoid alcohol, sedatives, or driving after taking.

---

## Contraindications

- Hypersensitivity to chlorpheniramine or other antihistamines
- Newborns and premature infants
- Breastfeeding women
- Narrow-angle glaucoma
- Severe liver disease or urinary retention (use with caution)

---

## Adverse Reactions

- **Common**
  - Drowsiness, dizziness
  - Dry mouth, constipation
  - Blurred vision

- **Uncommon**
  - Nausea, vomiting
  - Restlessness (especially in children)

- **Rare**
  - Confusion, hallucinations (elderly)
  - Allergic skin reaction

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

<Callout>
**Pregnancy:**
Use only if clearly needed.
Extensive experience suggests low risk when used short-term.

**Lactation:**
May pass into breast milk.
Avoid due to risk of infant drowsiness or irritability.
</Callout>
