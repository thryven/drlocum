<Callout>
First-generation antihistamine.
Relieves allergy symptoms and helps with short-term insomnia.
Has sedative and anticholinergic effects.
</Callout>

## Indication

- Allergic rhinitis, urticaria, pruritus
- Common cold symptoms
- Motion sickness prevention
- Short-term treatment of insomnia
- Adjunct in cough and cold preparations

- Not recommended for children under 2 years (risk of respiratory depression).

## Administration

- May be taken with or without food.
- Avoid alcohol and other sedatives.
- Do not drive or operate machinery after taking.
- Shake syrup well before use.

## Contraindications

- Hypersensitivity to diphenhydramine or similar antihistamines
- Neonates and premature infants
- Breastfeeding women
- Narrow-angle glaucoma
- Severe liver disease or urinary retention (use with caution)

## Adverse Reactions

- **Common**
  - Drowsiness, dizziness
  - Dry mouth, constipation
  - Blurred vision

- **Uncommon**
  - Headache, nausea
  - Nervousness or restlessness (especially in children)

- **Rare**
  - Confusion, hallucinations (elderly)
  - Hypotension, arrhythmia

## Pregnancy and Lactation

<Callout>
**Pregnancy:**
Extensive experience shows low risk in usual doses.
Avoid high or prolonged use near term (may affect newborn).

**Lactation:**
Passes into breast milk.
Avoid due to risk of infant sedation or irritability.
</Callout>
