<Callout>
  Prednisolone is a systemic glucocorticoid used for many inflammatory, allergic
  and autoimmune conditions. Doses range from low physiologic replacement to
  high immunosuppressive doses depending on indication.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Asthma exacerbations, severe allergic reactions, autoimmune flares, replacement therapy in adrenal insufficiency (dose adjustments required), and many other indications.

---

## Dose

- Highly indication-specific: short acute courses often use 20â€“40 mg daily for adults; some conditions require higher or tapering regimens. Paediatric dosing is weight-based.

---

## Administration

- Oral therapy commonly used for outpatient courses; IV/IM used in severe illness.

---

## Contraindications

<Alert type="error">
  - Systemic fungal infections and known hypersensitivity to prednisolone or
  excipients.
</Alert>

---

## Adverse Reactions

- Hyperglycaemia, immunosuppression, mood changes, weight gain, osteoporosis with long-term use, adrenal suppression.

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

- Use when clinically indicated; consider specialist advice for chronic therapy.
