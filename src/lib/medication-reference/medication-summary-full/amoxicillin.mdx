## Overview

Amoxicillin is a penicillin-type antibiotic used to treat a wide variety of bacterial infections. It works by stopping the growth of bacteria.

<Callout type="warning">
  **Allergy Alert:** Do not use if the patient has a known allergy to penicillin or other beta-lactam antibiotics.
</Callout>

---

## Indications

- **Otitis Media (Ear Infection)**: A common choice for middle ear infections.
- **Sinusitis**: Effective for bacterial sinus infections.
- **Streptococcal Pharyngitis (Strep Throat)**: A primary treatment option.
- **Pneumonia**: Community-acquired pneumonia.
- **Skin and Soft Tissue Infections**: Such as cellulitis.
- **Urinary Tract Infections**: In certain cases.

---

## Dosing

### Paediatric Dosing (Amoxicillin component)

- **Standard Dose**: 25-50 mg/kg/day divided every 8 to 12 hours.
- **High Dose (for resistant infections like otitis media)**: 80-90 mg/kg/day divided every 12 hours.
- **Maximum Dose**: Should not exceed the standard adult dose.

<Callout type="info">
  The quick reference calculator on this app uses a standard dose of 25 mg/kg/day divided twice daily for simplicity. Always adjust based on clinical indication and severity.
</Callout>

### Adult Dosing

- **Standard Dose**: 250-500 mg every 8 hours, or 500-875 mg every 12 hours.

---

## Contraindications

- **Penicillin Allergy**: History of anaphylaxis or severe skin reaction to any penicillin.
- **Mononucleosis**: Increased risk of developing a rash.

## Pregnancy & Lactation

- **Pregnancy**: Generally considered safe for use during pregnancy (Category B).
- **Lactation**: Excreted in breast milk in small amounts; compatible with breastfeeding, but monitor infant for rash or diarrhoea.

---

## Important Notes

- **Administration**: May be taken with or without food. Taking with food may reduce gastrointestinal side effects.
- **Complete the Course**: Instruct patients to complete the full course of antibiotics, even if they feel better, to prevent the development of resistant bacteria.
