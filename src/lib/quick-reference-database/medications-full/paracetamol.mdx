<Callout>
Analgesic and antipyretic agent.
Mechanism involves central inhibition of prostaglandin synthesis.
Weak anti-inflammatory properties compared to NSAIDs.
Overdose carries significant risk of hepatotoxicity.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Treatment of **mild to moderate pain**.
- Reduction of **fever (pyrexia)**.
- Common painful and febrile conditions, e.g., headache, toothache, colds, influenza.
- Suitable for patients who cannot tolerate aspirin or NSAIDs.

## Administration

- May be taken **with or without food**.

---

## Contraindications

<Alert type='error'>
- **Hypersensitivity** to paracetamol or any excipient.
- **Severe hepatic impairment** or active liver disease.
- Risk of **fatal overdose** and liver failure exists if recommended maximum doses are exceeded.
</Alert>

- **Caution** is required in:
  - Renal impairment (eGFR less than 30 mL/min/1.73m2): minimum 6-hour dosing interval recommended.
  - Alcohol dependence or chronic excessive alcohol intake.
  - Chronic malnutrition, low body weight (less than 50 kg), or chronic dehydration (consider dose reduction).
  - Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency.
- Caution if taking other hepatotoxic drugs or liver enzyme inducers (e.g., carbamazepine, phenytoin).

---

## Adverse Reactions

Common (≥1%):

- Generally well tolerated at recommended doses
- Rarely, hypersensitivity reactions (rash, urticaria)

Uncommon/Rare (Requires immediate cessation and medical review):

- Severe skin reactions: Stevens-Johnson syndrome (SJS), Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN), Acute Generalised Exanthematous Pustulosis (AGEP)
- Hypersensitivity/Anaphylaxis (angioedema, dyspnoea)
- Haematological disorders (e.g., thrombocytopenia, leucopenia, agranulocytosis) - very rare
- Liver damage (hepatotoxicity), especially with overdose

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

<Callout type='warning'>
**Pregnancy:**
Paracetamol is the **preferred analgesic/antipyretic** in pregnancy.
Use at the **lowest effective dose** for the **shortest duration** necessary.
Do not use in combination with other analgesics without medical advice.

**Lactation:**
Excreted in **small, clinically insignificant amounts** in breast milk.
Considered **safe** for use by breastfeeding mothers at recommended doses.
</Callout>
