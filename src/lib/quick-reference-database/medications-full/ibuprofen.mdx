<Callout>
  Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) with analgesic, antipyretic and
  anti-inflammatory effects. Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest
  duration.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Mild to moderate pain (musculoskeletal pain, dysmenorrhoea, dental pain).
- Fever where antipyretic therapy is indicated.

---

## Dose

### Adults

- Oral: 200–400 mg every 4–6 hours as required; OTC maximum commonly 1.2 g/day, prescription doses may be higher (up to 2.4 g/day) under medical supervision.

### Paediatrics

- Weight-based dosing: typically 5–10 mg/kg per dose (maximum 40 mg/kg/day). Follow product-specific guidance.

---

## Administration

- Take with food or milk to reduce gastrointestinal irritation.

---

## Contraindications

<Alert type="error">
  - Known hypersensitivity to ibuprofen or other NSAIDs. - Active or recurrent
  peptic ulcer disease or gastrointestinal bleeding. - Severe hepatic, renal or
  cardiac failure.
</Alert>

Use caution in patients with asthma, cardiovascular disease, renal impairment, older people, and when co-prescribed with anticoagulants.

---

## Adverse Reactions

- Common: dyspepsia, nausea, abdominal pain.
- Serious: gastrointestinal bleeding, renal impairment, hypersensitivity reactions.

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

- Avoid in late pregnancy due to risk of premature closure of the ductus arteriosus. Use with caution in early pregnancy if necessary.
