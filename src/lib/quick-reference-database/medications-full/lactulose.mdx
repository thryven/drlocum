<Callout>
  Lactulose is an osmotic laxative used for constipation and in hepatic
  encephalopathy to reduce ammonia absorption.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Constipation and management of hepatic encephalopathy as part of broader therapy.

---

## Dose

- Adults (constipation): typically 15–30 mL once daily, titrating to effect (often 10–20 g lactulose daily). Adjust dose to produce 2–3 soft stools per day.
- Hepatic encephalopathy: higher or titrated dosing often required; follow specialist guidance.

---

## Administration

- Oral syrup; mix with water or fruit juice if desired. Titrate dose according to bowel habit.

---

## Contraindications

<Alert type="error">
  - Galactosaemia (if syrup formulation contains lactose/galactose), allergy to
  components.
</Alert>

---

## Adverse Reactions

- Flatulence, abdominal discomfort, diarrhoea if overdosed.

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

- Considered compatible with pregnancy when clinically indicated; consult product SmPC.
