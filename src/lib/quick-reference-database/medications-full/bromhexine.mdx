## Indication

- Symptomatic relief of productive cough with viscid/tenacious sputum (acute and chronic bronchitis).
- Adjunct to physiotherapy in respiratory conditions with excessive or thick secretions.

## Administration

- Oral formulations (tablets, syrup) — take with a glass of water; may be given with food to minimise gastrointestinal upset.
- Follow the specific product instructions for formulation/age-appropriate preparations.

---

## Contraindications and Cautions

- **Contraindications:** Known hypersensitivity to bromhexine or any excipients.
- **Cautions:**

  - Not recommended in infants/children under 2 years; follow local regulatory guidance for younger children.
  - Use with caution in patients with severe hepatic impairment — metabolised hepatically; consider specialist advice.
  - Caution in patients with peptic ulcer disease or gastro-oesophageal reflux due to potential for increased secretions.

---

## Adverse Reactions

- **Common:** Gastrointestinal upset (nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea), abdominal pain, dyspepsia.
- **Uncommon / Rare:** Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, urticaria), bronchospasm (rare), headache, dizziness.
- **Very rare / serious:** Hepatic dysfunction and cholestatic jaundice have been reported rarely; stop drug if unexplained liver enzyme elevations or jaundice occur.

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

<Callout type='warning'>
**Pregnancy:** Data are limited. Bromhexine should be used in pregnancy only if clinically indicated and after considering risk–benefit.

**Lactation:** Small amounts may be excreted in breast milk. Use with caution and consider monitoring the infant for any adverse effects; alternatives may be preferred for nursing mothers.
</Callout>
