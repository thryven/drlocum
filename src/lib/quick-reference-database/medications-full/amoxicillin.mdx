<Callout>
  A beta-lactam antibiotic (aminopenicillin) commonly used for a wide range of
  bacterial infections. Use according to local susceptibility patterns and
  stewardship principles.
</Callout>

## Indication

- Respiratory tract infections
- otitis media, sinusitis
- skin and soft tissue infections
- urinary tract infections

## Dose

### Adults

- 500 mg every 8 hours

### Paediatrics

- 25â€“50 mg/kg/day in divided doses; higher doses may be used for severe infections.

## Administration

- Take with or without food.

## Contraindications

<Alert type="error">
  - History of penicillin hypersensitivity (immediate or delayed reactions).
</Alert>

Use with caution in patients with mononucleosis (risk of rash) and severe renal impairment (dose adjustments may be required).

## Adverse Reactions

- Common: diarrhoea, nausea, rash.
- Serious: severe hypersensitivity (anaphylaxis), Clostridioides difficile infection.

## Pregnancy and Lactation

- Generally considered safe in pregnancy and lactation when indicated; follow local guidance.
