<Callout>
  Dexamethasone is a potent glucocorticoid with anti-inflammatory and
  immunosuppressant effects used in a wide range of conditions. Dose and
  duration depend on the indication.
</Callout>

---

## Indication

- Cerebral oedema, severe allergic reactions, some inflammatory and autoimmune conditions, antiemetic adjunct in chemotherapy, and other specialist indications.

---

## Dose

- Dosing is highly indication-specific: single doses of 4–8 mg may be used for nausea/antiemetic effect; higher doses (e.g., 6–10 mg or more) used for severe inflammation or cerebral oedema as advised by specialists.

---

## Administration

- Oral, IV or IM routes depending on indication and formulation.

---

## Contraindications

<Alert type="error">
  - Systemic fungal infections and known hypersensitivity to dexamethasone or
  excipients.
</Alert>

Use caution with infections, uncontrolled diabetes, active peptic ulcer disease, and in patients at risk of adrenal suppression.

---

## Adverse Reactions

- Hyperglycaemia, increased infection risk, mood changes, insomnia, fluid retention, adrenal suppression with prolonged use.

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

- Use with specialist advice when required; balance maternal benefit against potential fetal risks.
