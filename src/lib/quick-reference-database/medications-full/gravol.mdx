<Alert>
Antihistamine with antiemetic effect.
Used to prevent and treat motion sickness, nausea, and vertigo.
</Alert>

---

## Indication

- Prevention and treatment of motion sickness
- Nausea and vomiting due to inner ear disorders
- Vertigo from vestibular causes

---

## Dose

### Adults

- 50–100 mg every 4–6 hours as needed
- Maximum: 400 mg per day

## Administration

- Take 30–60 minutes before travel to prevent motion sickness.
- Can be taken with food or milk to reduce stomach upset.
- Avoid alcohol or other sedatives.

---

## Contraindications

- Hypersensitivity to dimenhydrinate or diphenhydramine
- Narrow-angle glaucoma
- Severe liver disease
- Children less than 2 years (without medical advice)

---

## Adverse Reactions

- **Common**
  - Drowsiness, dizziness
  - Dry mouth, blurred vision
  - Constipation

- **Less common**
  - Urinary retention
  - Confusion (especially in elderly)
  - Palpitations

---

## Pregnancy and Lactation

<Alert >
**Pregnancy:**
Generally considered safe when used occasionally. Widely used for morning sickness.

**Lactation:**
Small amounts may pass into breast milk. Use with caution — may cause infant drowsiness.
</Alert>
