An Overview of Lymphoma: Diagnosis and Treatment

Understanding lymphoma, a cancer of the lymphatic system.

What is Lymphoma?

Lymphoma is a type of cancer that begins in the infection-fighting cells of the immune system, called lymphocytes. These cells are in the lymph nodes, spleen, thymus, bone marrow, and other parts of the body. When you have lymphoma, lymphocytes change and grow out of control.

There are two main types of lymphoma:

  • Hodgkin Lymphoma: Marked by the presence of a specific type of cell called the Reed-Sternberg cell.
  • Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (NHL): This is the more common type and includes a large, diverse group of cancers of immune system cells.

Signs and Symptoms

The most common sign of lymphoma is a painless swelling of lymph nodes in the neck, armpits, or groin. Other symptoms may include:

  • Persistent fatigue
  • Fever and chills
  • Night sweats
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Shortness of breath
  • Itchy skin

Diagnosis

If lymphoma is suspected, a doctor will perform several tests to confirm the diagnosis and determine the type and stage of the cancer.

  1. Physical Exam: Checking for swollen lymph nodes, spleen, or liver.
  2. Biopsy: A lymph node biopsy is the definitive way to diagnose lymphoma. A small piece of tissue is removed and examined under a microscope for cancer cells.
  3. Imaging Tests: CT scans, PET scans, and MRIs can help determine the extent of the lymphoma.
  4. Blood Tests: To check blood cell counts and organ function.

Treatment Options

Treatment for lymphoma depends on the type and stage of the disease, as well as the patient's overall health. Common treatments include:

  • Chemotherapy: The use of drugs to kill cancer cells.
  • Radiation Therapy: Using high-energy beams to target and destroy cancer cells.
  • Immunotherapy: Using the body's own immune system to fight cancer. This includes treatments like monoclonal antibodies.
  • Targeted Therapy: Drugs that target specific vulnerabilities in cancer cells.
  • Stem Cell Transplant: Allows for higher doses of chemotherapy. It may be an option for recurrent lymphoma.
